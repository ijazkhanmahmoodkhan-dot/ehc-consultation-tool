Built-in Counselling & Governance Reference

TopicInformation to cover
Copper IUDMost effective EC. Can be fitted up to 5 days after UPSI, or later depending on earliest likely ovulation. Provides ongoing contraception. Not affected by weight/BMI or enzyme-inducing medicines. Refer urgently if accepted, oral EC outside window, enzyme-inducer use, weight/BMI concern, multiple UPSI, likely ovulation, or oral EC unsuitable.
Ulipristal acetate 30mgSingle oral dose up to 120 hours after UPSI. Generally more effective than LNG, especially closer to ovulation. Not recommended with enzyme-inducing medicines used in the last 4 weeks. Avoid where excluded by PGD/SmPC, including severe asthma treated with oral glucocorticoids. Delay hormonal contraception for 5 days after UPA, then use condoms until the method is effective. Breastfeeding: avoid breastfeeding for 1 week and express/discard milk as per product information.
LevonorgestrelUsually 1.5mg single dose as soon as possible within 72 hours. Local PGD/guidance may support 3mg in enzyme-inducer situations or weight/BMI concern. Hormonal contraception can usually be started/restarted immediately with additional barrier precautions according to method.
VomitingIf vomiting occurs within the product-specific time window, repeat dose or refer according to PGD/product information. Consider copper IUD referral if vomiting risk persists.
Pregnancy testingAdvise pregnancy test if period is more than 7 days late, abnormal, lighter/shorter, or no bleed within 3 weeks. EC does not protect against pregnancy from future UPSI.
Safety-nettingUrgent medical help for severe lower abdominal pain, shoulder tip pain, fainting, heavy bleeding, or ectopic pregnancy symptoms. Discuss STI testing, condoms, regular contraception, sexual health clinic, GP and safeguarding support.
